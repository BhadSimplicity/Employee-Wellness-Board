**Remote Work Employee Wellness Analysis**

> An end-to-end HR Analytics project that explores the impact of remote work on employee mental health, work-life balance, stress levels, job satisfaction, and overall well-being using Microsoft Excel and Power BI.

**Project Overview**

The rapid adoption of remote and hybrid work has transformed the modern workplace. While these work arrangements offer flexibility and increased autonomy, they have also introduced challenges such as burnout, social isolation, stress, and declining work-life balance.

This project analyzes employee wellness data to identify trends and relationships between workplace factors and mental health outcomes. The resulting interactive Power BI dashboard provides actionable insights that can help Human Resource professionals and organizational leaders make informed decisions to improve employee well-being and productivity. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0} :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

**Project Objectives**

The project aims to:

- Identify factors influencing employees' mental health.
- Analyze relationships between workload, work-life balance, and wellness.
- Examine how job satisfaction relates to stress, burnout, and company support.
- Explore employee distribution across different work locations.
- Provide actionable recommendations for improving employee well-being. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

**Business Questions**

This analysis answers several important questions:

- Which mental health conditions are most common among employees?
- How does remote work affect employee well-being?
- Is work-life balance associated with job satisfaction?
- Does sleep quality influence employee satisfaction?
- Which work locations have the highest employee representation?
- Are employees experiencing social isolation?
- How does company support impact job satisfaction?

**Key Insights**

Analysis of **2,544 employees** revealed several notable findings:

- Employees are almost evenly distributed across Remote, Hybrid, and Onsite work arrangements.
- Burnout, Depression, and Anxiety represent a significant proportion of reported mental health conditions.
- Employee representation across global regions is balanced, indicating a diverse workforce.
- Most employees reported moderate to high levels of social isolation.
- Work-life balance ratings are generally moderate rather than excellent.
- Sleep quality appears to be associated with employee satisfaction.
- Most employees reported being Neutral or Somewhat Satisfied with their jobs, suggesting opportunities for increased engagement.

**Recommendations**

Based on the analysis, organizations should consider:

- Expanding employee mental wellness programs.
- Increasing virtual engagement activities for remote workers.
- Encouraging healthier work-hour boundaries.
- Improving recognition and career development initiatives.
- Investing in employee support systems that enhance work-life balance.

These initiatives can help reduce burnout, improve employee satisfaction, and foster a healthier workplace culture. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
